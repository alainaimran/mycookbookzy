Chicken Marsala

Chicken Marsala is a savory Italian-American dish featuring tender pan-fried chicken cutlets in a rich, mushroom Marsala wine sauce. It's a perfect combination of flavors, ideal for a weeknight meal or special occasions.

Step by Step Recipe

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (pounded thin)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (for dredging)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 8 oz mushrooms (sliced)
  • 3/4 cup Marsala wine
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth

    • Instructions

    1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then dredge in flour.
    2. Heat olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté the chicken until golden brown on both sides, then remove and set aside.
    3. In the same skillet, add the mushrooms and cook until soft.
    4. Add the Marsala wine and chicken broth to the pan, scraping up the browned bits.
    5. Simmer the sauce for a few minutes until slightly reduced.
    6. Return the chicken to the pan and cook for a few more minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and coat the chicken.
    7. Garnish with parsley and serve.
