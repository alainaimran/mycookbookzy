Simple Dumplings Recipe

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup hot water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup ground meat (pork, chicken, or beef)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, or onions)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

1. Make the Dough

  1. Mix the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the hot water while stirring with a fork or chopsticks until the dough starts to come together.
  2. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough is sticky, add a little more flour as needed.
  3. Rest the Dough: Cover the kneaded dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling

  1. Mix the Filling: In a bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped vegetables, soy sauce, sesame oil (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly combined.

3. Form the Dumplings

  1. Roll Out the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into small portions (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each portion into a thin circle (about 3 inches in diameter) on a floured surface.
  2. Fill the Dumplings: Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can make sealing difficult.
  3. Seal the Dumplings: Moisten the edges of the dough with a little water, fold it in half over the filling, and pinch the edges to seal. You can also make pleats for a decorative touch.

4. Cook the Dumplings

  1. Boil: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the dumplings into the boiling water. Cook for about 5-7 minutes or until they float to the surface and are cooked through.
  2. Steam (Alternative Method): Place the dumplings in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Steam over boiling water for about 10-12 minutes.

5. Serve

  • Serve the dumplings hot with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, vinegar, and chili oil if desired.

Tips

  • Dipping Sauce: Mix soy sauce with rice vinegar and add a dash of chili oil for extra flavor.
  • Freezing: Uncooked dumplings can be frozen on a baking sheet and then stored in a zip-top bag for future use. Cook them from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.
  • Variations: Feel free to experiment with different fillings, such as tofu, shrimp, or different vegetables according to your taste.
