Beef Wellington

Beef Wellington is a decadent and impressive dish that combines a tender beef fillet with layers of flavors, all encased in a flaky puff pastry.It's often served with a red wine reduction sauce or a rich demi-glace, making it a luxurious centerpiece for special occasions.

Step by Step Recipe

Ingredients

  • Beef tenderloin (center-cut)
  • Mushrooms (for duxelles)
  • Shallots, garlic, and herbs
  • Pâté (optional) or prosciutto
  • Egg wash (for sealing the pastry)
  • Salt, pepper, olive oil
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Dijon mustard (optional)
  • Puff pastry
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

    • Instructions

    1. Season the beef fillet with salt and pepper.
    2. Sear the beef on all sides in a hot pan until browned.
    3. Remove the beef from the heat and brush with Dijon mustard; allow it to cool.
    4. Finely chop mushrooms, shallots, and garlic, then sauté until moisture evaporates to form a paste (duxelles).
    5. Lay slices of prosciutto on plastic wrap and spread the duxelles over the prosciutto.
    6. Place the beef on the prosciutto and roll it tightly using the plastic wrap, then refrigerate for 15 minutes. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface large enough to wrap the beef.
    7. Remove the beef from the plastic wrap and place it on the puff pastry, then wrap the pastry around it, sealing the edges with egg wash. Brush the outside of the puff pastry with egg wash.
    8. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and bake for 25-30 minutes until the pastry is golden brown. Let the Beef Wellington rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
