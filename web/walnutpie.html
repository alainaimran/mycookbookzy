Walnut Pie Recipe

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

For the Walnut Filling:

  • 1 cup walnuts, chopped (or whole if you prefer)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light corn syrup (or maple syrup)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and mix with a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    2. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together. Do not overmix.
    3. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
    4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). On a floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch pie pan. Place the dough in the pie pan and trim any excess. Poke holes in the bottom with a fork to prevent bubbling.
    5. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for about 10-15 minutes. Remove the weights and parchment, then bake for an additional 5 minutes until lightly golden. Set aside to cool.
  2. Make the Walnut Filling:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped walnuts, sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Stir until well combined.
  3. Assemble and Bake:
    1. Pour the walnut filling into the pre-baked pie crust, spreading it evenly.
    2. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly puffed. The center may still be slightly jiggly but will firm up as it cools.
  4. Cool and Serve:
    1. Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack for at least 1 hour before slicing.
    2. Serve the walnut pie at room temperature, optionally topped with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Tips

  • Storage: Store any leftovers covered at room temperature for up to 3 days, or refrigerate for up to a week.
  • Additions: You can add chocolate chips or a splash of bourbon to the filling for added flavor.
  • Nut Variations: You can mix walnuts with pecans or other nuts if desired.
