Shakshuka Recipe

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (or 4-5 fresh tomatoes, chopped)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, for garnish

For the Eggs:

  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Feta cheese (optional, for topping)

Instructions

1. Cook the Vegetables:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, and saut√© until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

2. Make the Sauce:

  1. Add the ground cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  2. Pour in the diced tomatoes (with their juices) and season with salt, pepper, and sugar.
  3. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and flavors meld. Stir occasionally.

3. Poach the Eggs:

  1. Make small wells in the sauce and carefully crack an egg into each well.
  2. Cover the skillet and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny. Cook longer if you prefer firmer yolks.

4. Serve:

  1. Sprinkle crumbled feta cheese and chopped parsley or cilantro over the top.
  2. Serve hot with crusty bread or pita for dipping.

Tips

  • Customization: Feel free to add spinach, olives, or other vegetables to the sauce for extra flavor and nutrition.
  • Spiciness: Adjust the amount of cayenne pepper based on your heat preference.
