Truffle Risotto

Truffle Risotto is a creamy and indulgent dish made with Arborio rice cooked in broth, enhanced by the earthy richness of truffle oil. Topped with Parmesan cheese and optionally shaved truffles, it offers a gourmet experience perfect for special occasions.

Step by Step Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice (risotto rice)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons butter (divided)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth (warmed)
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 1 tablespoon truffle oil (or more to taste)
  • Shaved truffles (optional, for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)

    • Instructions

    1. Prepare the broth: Warm the chicken or vegetable broth in a saucepan over low heat and keep it warm throughout the cooking process.
    2. Sauté the onions: In a large pan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
    3. Toast the rice: Add the Arborio rice to the pan with the onions and stir it until it becomes lightly toasted (2-3 minutes). The rice should absorb the flavors from the butter and onions.
    4. Deglaze with wine: Pour in the white wine and cook until it has mostly evaporated, stirring frequently.
    5. Cook the risotto: Begin adding the warm broth, one ladle at a time, allowing the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. Stir frequently to help release the starches in the rice, which creates the creamy texture of risotto. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes until the rice is tender but still slightly firm (al dente).
    6. Add the cheese and truffle oil: Once the rice is cooked, stir in the remaining butter, Parmesan cheese, and truffle oil. Adjust salt and pepper to taste.
    7. Garnish: For an extra touch of luxury, top with shaved truffles and fresh parsley before serving.
