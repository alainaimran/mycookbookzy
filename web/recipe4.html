Baked Alaska

Baked Alaska is a show-stopping dessert with layers of cake, ice cream, and a toasted meringue exterior. The dish is known for its dramatic contrast of cold ice cream inside and warm, browned meringue on the outside.

Step by Step Recipe

Ingredients

  • Cake Base: 1 prepared sponge cake or pound cake (cut into a circle)
  • Ice Cream: 1 quart of your favorite flavor (slightly softened)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    • Instructions

    1. Prepare the Cake Base: Place the cake base on a baking sheet. You can use a pre-made or homemade sponge cake.
    2. Shape the Ice Cream: Place a large, rounded mound of softened ice cream on top of the cake and smooth it into a dome shape. Freeze the cake and ice cream until firm (about 1-2 hours).
    3. Make the Meringue: Whisk egg whites with cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar while continuing to whisk until stiff, glossy peaks form. Add vanilla extract.
    4. Assemble the Baked Alaska: Remove the cake and ice cream from the freezer. Cover the entire dessert with a thick layer of meringue, making sure no ice cream is visible. The meringue should completely insulate the ice cream to prevent melting.
    5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 500°F (260°C). Bake the Baked Alaska for 3-5 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown. You can also use a kitchen torch to brown the meringue.
    6. Serve: Serve immediately, slicing into the dessert to reveal the layers of cake, ice cream, and toasted meringue.
