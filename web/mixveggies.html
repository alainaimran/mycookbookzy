Mixed Vegetables Recipe

Ingredients

Vegetables:

  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup bell peppers (red, yellow, or green), chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup snap peas or green beans
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)

For Cooking:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (like parsley or cilantro) for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and chop all the vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Make sure they are roughly the same size for even cooking.
  2. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or wok, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add the minced garlic and ginger (if using) to the skillet and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Cook the Vegetables: Add the carrots first and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, as they take longer to cook. Next, add the bell peppers, broccoli, cauliflower, and snap peas (or green beans). Stir-fry for another 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp. Finally, add the corn and continue to cook for another 2 minutes until everything is heated through.
  5. Season: Season the vegetables with salt, pepper, soy sauce, and lemon juice (if using). Stir well to combine and let cook for another minute.
  6. Serve: Remove from heat and garnish with fresh herbs if desired. Serve hot as a side dish or over rice or noodles for a complete meal.

Tips

  • Customization: Feel free to swap in any of your favorite vegetables or whatever you have on hand. Zucchini, asparagus, or mushrooms are great additions.
  • Spice It Up: Add red pepper flakes or a splash of hot sauce for a bit of heat.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the vegetables in advance and store them in the refrigerator. Just sauté them right before serving.
